Dear Editor,

We submit for your consideration a 12-page regular paper proposing a
five-engine architecture for agentic, context-aware risk prediction in
the Internet of Value, and grounding the proposal in two fully-public
empirical artefacts: a 27-hour on-chain liquidity-defence cycle that
holds a constitutional role contract under two stop-loss events and
three out-of-band human approvals, and a 168-hour production
prediction-router calibration arc that we report with explicit
class-imbalance honesty (53% live-API baseline; 99.34% on a
top-cap-restricted 168-hour cohort; Brier 0.1335).

The paper sits at the intersection of three IEEE TIFS-relevant strands:
(i) cross-chain bridge security (anchored to the IEEE S\&P 2024 SoK and
the canonical pre-2024 ACM Computing Surveys interoperability survey);
(ii) agentic-AI safety, with the agentic engine framed under the
constitutional-AI paradigm and the orchestration runtime separating
agent-task management from runtime orchestration to preserve a verifiable
audit trail; and (iii) decentralised verification, with a Bittensor
shadow validator deployed on testnet (5,097 successful rounds, 0 auth
failures, 11 autonomous Auth0 token refreshes, ~57 hours) and the
falsifiable validator-loss decomposition stated formally.

We confirm: (a) the manuscript is not under review at any other venue;
(b) we will post a parallel arXiv preprint under cs.CR (primary) and
q-fin.RM (cross-list), consistent with TIFS's preprint-coexistence
policy; (c) all empirical numbers in the manuscript are anchored to
primary-source artefacts (decision log, calibration manifest, soak
telemetry) committed in the same git tree as the manuscript; (d) the
on-chain experimental defence policy operated only on the corresponding
author's non-treasury wallet, did not act on third-party funds, and is
documented in a public role contract.
